    The story behind

    Maker's Note

    Kandangi is a village near Karaikudi in the Sivaganga district of Tamil Nadu — the heartland of the Chettinad region. The Chettinad check saree takes its name from here and was originally woven for the women of the Nattukotai Chettiars, a mercantile community whose elaborate mansions and rich textile tradition define the region. The large bold check is distinctive to Kandangi — most Chettinad sarees use a smaller check — and the copper zari border is a regional signature not found in check sarees from other weaving centres.
